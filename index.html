Introduction

The Future Founders' Challenge

The Future Founders' Challenge (FFC) is a student-led national entrepreneurship and leadership competition founded at POWIIS, Penang. Inspired by the spirit of global business challenges such as DECA, it is designed to equip secondary and pre-university students across Malaysia with the skills needed to excel in innovation, leadership, collaboration, resilience, and social impact.

FFC features three competitive academic events — Business Debate, Case Study Analysis, and the Innovators' Challenge. It provides participants with recognised awards and certified achievements that enhance university and scholarship applications. Top-performing teams may represent Malaysia in international virtual competitions, connecting them with a global network of young innovators.

Vision

Confidence, Creativity, and Leadership

To give students in Malaysia a chance to explore business in a real, hands-on way. We want to build confidence, creativity, and leadership through a challenge that's fun but also helps learn real-world skills.

It's not just about winning — it's about preparing for the future, and showing that students at POWIIS can think big and lead with purpose.

"Do not be afraid to take bold ideas, question assumptions, and learn from the process. The goal is not only to win, but to grow."

— Darren Chan, Founder & Executive Director

Preparation

FFC
Resources

The FFC Resource Book is a structured collection of business knowledge designed specifically for students preparing for the Future Founders Challenge. It is not a textbook, and it is not meant for memorisation — it is a tool to help students think, analyse, and make decisions like real entrepreneurs.

View Resource Book
Core Purpose

This resource trains students to break down complex business situations, identify root problems instead of surface issues, compare strategic options, and justify decisions with clear reasoning.

In FFC, success does not come from knowing definitions. It comes from being able to analyse a situation and explain why one approach is better than another.

What It Contains
Business models — B2B, Marketplace, Platform, Freemium, and more
Ownership structures and funding strategies
Marketing, customer strategies, and operational decisions
Real-world case studies and conflicting strategic trade-offs

Students who read it passively may understand the content. Students who read it actively — questioning, analysing, and reflecting — will develop the ability to apply that knowledge effectively. In FFC, that difference is what separates participation from winning.

Event 01 — Business Debate

Business
Debate

Strategy. Persuasion. Commercial Judgement.

01
What is it?

The Art of the Business Argument

The FFC Business Debate is a structured, competitive discussion where two teams present and defend their business solutions to the same case study. Rather than simply proposing ideas, teams must justify their strategy, demonstrate how it works in practice, and critically evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of opposing solutions.

Each team consists of three speakers with defined roles. The debate tests strategic thinking, business reasoning, communication skills, and the ability to respond under pressure.

Judging Focus
Depth of Analysis
How deeply does the team understand the business problem?
Feasibility
Are the proposed solutions realistic and financially grounded?
Communication
Clarity, evidence use, persuasion, and professional delivery.
Official Procedure

How It Works

Phase 01
Orientation
& Setup
Getting Into the Room

Teams convene in the auditorium to receive their Debate Tree, which details assigned rooms, opponents, and stance (Affirmative or Negative) for each round. Upon entering the debate room, teams introduce themselves to the adjudicator. This is the only time electronic devices may be used for research.

Phase 02
Preparation
15 Minutes to Build Your Case

The adjudicator reveals the specific business case or motion. Teams have a 15-minute block to map out their strategy, draft speeches, and find evidence. After this window, all electronic devices must be put away — all following notes must be on paper.

Phase 03
The Debate
Round
Six Speakers. Alternating. Under Pressure.

The debate follows a strict alternating order. Each speaker has up to 4 minutes to deliver their points. Between each speech, both teams receive a 60-second buffer to adjust rebuttals.

1st Leader speaks (4 min)
1st Challenger responds (4 min)
2nd Leader speaks (4 min)
2nd Challenger responds (4 min)
3rd Leader speaks (4 min)
3rd Challenger responds (4 min)
Phase 04
Feedback
& Results
Decision & Debrief

Following the final speech, teams have 90 seconds to prepare a 90-second constructive feedback session for opponents. The adjudicator then deliberates and announces the winner based on depth of business analysis, feasibility, and communication effectiveness.

Key Rules

Regulations

One Point of Information (POI) allowed per student, directed at their direct counterpart in the opposing team.
Speeches are recorded. ChatGPT is allowed only during the initial 15-minute research phase. Final speeches must be the students' original work — AI detectors will be used during review.
The Debate Tree determines team progression. Bull path = win. Bear path = loss. Teams are assigned Affirmative or Negative stances per round.
Professional conduct must be maintained at all times. Disrespect to opponents or judges may result in immediate disqualification.
Glossary

Key Terms

Leader
Defends or supports the motion
Challenger
Opposes the motion
Bull Path
Advancement after a win
Bear Path
Redirection after a loss
Team Code
Unique ID (e.g. A067) for tracking
Match Progression

The Debate Tree

The Debate Tree is FFC's structured match progression system — it visually maps how teams move through debate rounds based on performance, ensuring fairness, strategy, and competitive balance across all three rounds.

Debate Tree Diagram
Place FFC_DebateTree.png in the same folder as this HTML file
Round 1

Each team starts at their assigned room with a designated Leader or Challenger role. Room codes (e.g. A102) are shown on the tree.

Round 2 Pathing

Win → Bull path, stay in or advance to the next room. Loss → Bear path, move to the alternative room and switch role.

Round 3 Finals

Your Round 2 result sends you to a final room. Overall standings are calculated from performance across all three rounds.

Event 02 — Case Study Analysis

Case Study
Analysis

Diagnose. Analyse. Solve. Under pressure.

02
What is it?

Executive
Thinking Under
a Time Limit

The FFC Case Study Analysis is a high-pressure competitive event that challenges teams to diagnose and solve complex, real-world business problems within a strict timeframe.

Participants are judged on their ability to identify underlying issues, analyse root causes with logical precision, and develop strategic solutions that demonstrate both financial awareness and risk control.

This event serves as a rigorous test of a team's analytical thinking, structured reasoning, and professional presentation skills — requiring teams to critically evaluate their own proposals to ensure they are both realistic and effective.

Official Event Flow

How It Works

Phase 01
Setup
Room Requirements
3 tables and 3 chairs per team at each station.
Judge must have a clear, unobstructed view of all device screens at all times. Repositioning to hide screens is grounds for disqualification.
Teams must grant the judge viewing access to all digital slides and documents before starting.
Phase 02
Preparation
35 Minutes of Prep

Phase A (5 min): Reading and initial analysis. Research is allowed; writing may begin. Phase B (30 min): Full case study preparation — build your strategy and slides. All devices must be turned off when the timer hits zero, except for the presenting team.

AI Rules
Strictly Prohibited: ChatGPT, AI writing tools, auto-analysis software, AI browser extensions.
Allowed: Google Search, YouTube, Wikipedia, Google Translate (translation only), QuillBot (grammar only).
Phase 03
Presentation
15 Minutes Per Team

Each team has 15 minutes to present their analysis. The non-presenting team must remain silent, face the presenters, and refrain from note-taking or further preparation.

Phase 04
Q&A
10 Minutes — 2 Questions
Exactly 2 questions per team (strictly enforced).
If the opposing team asks 1 question (max 1 min), the judge asks 1. If the opposing team asks 0, the judge asks 2.
1 minute to ask; 4 minutes to answer. Total event duration: 90 minutes.
Scoring Breakdown

The 25-Star
System

Each of the five sections is worth up to 5 stars. Q&A bonus stars (up to +6) may be awarded on top.

Clarity of Vision

Executive Summary and Product Overview. Compelling value proposition with a clearly defined target audience.

Problem–Solution Fit

Does the product actually fix the identified problem? Show deep understanding of the root cause with originality.

Business Structure

Business model and team roles. Clear hierarchy with justification for each leadership role and skill set.

Marketing & Operations

Customer Acquisition Model and detailed Operational Workflow — supply chain, staffing, and scalability.

Financial Planning

Realistic revenue projections. Clear revenue model, profit path, and demonstrated understanding of risks.

Award
The Analytical Excellence Award

Given to the team that delivers the most well-reasoned and strategic solution to a complex business challenge. Winners are recognised for their ability to analyse, adapt, and provide actionable recommendations under pressure. This award recognises those who think like executives — using logic, insight, and quick reasoning to lead through uncertainty.

Event 03 — Innovators' Challenge

Innovators'
Challenge

Build a business from a theme in two hours. Then defend it.

03
What is it?

From Theme to Business Plan in 2 Hours

Teams receive a challenge theme at the Opening Ceremony and use the associated case study to build a complete business plan within a 2-hour preparation period. After lunch, they present to a panel of three judges for 10 to 30 minutes, followed by a structured Q&A.

This event develops creative problem-solving, entrepreneurial thinking, and the ability to turn ideas into realistic, impactful solutions under strict time pressure.

Official Event Flow

How It Works

Phase 01
Preparation
2-Hour Development Period

Teams gather for a central briefing at the Opening Ceremony where the official challenge theme is introduced. Teams then move to assigned rooms with 3 judges. Documents must be shared with all 3 judges and the Judging Email before preparation begins.

Allowed
AI tools for research and calculations · Team discussion · Laptops and tablets · Taking notes
Prohibited
Copying AI-generated content · Outside help · Hiding screens · Disabling document sharing
Phase 02
Presentation
10–30 Minutes Per Team
Minimum 10 minutes, maximum 30 minutes of speaking time.
Teams must clearly identify the problem, target market, and propose an innovative, practical, and scalable solution.
Non-presenting teams must close devices, not edit work, and remain silent and respectful.
Presentation order is determined by ascending Team ID number.
Phase 03
Q&A
10 Minutes — Strategic Testing
Judge A and Judge B each ask 1 question based on their rubric area.
1 minute to ask; 4 minutes to answer. No follow-up questions allowed.
Questions assess feasibility, risks, cost structure, and sustainability.
Phase 04
Results
Room Winner Announcement

After all presentations, judges calculate scores and announce the winning team in each room. The overall winner across all rooms (highest total stars) is announced at the Closing Ceremony. Total time per team: up to 40 minutes including Q&A.

FAQ

Frequently Asked
Questions

Who can participate in FFC?
+
FFC is open to secondary and pre-university students across Malaysia. Students must compete in teams of 3. All participants must be enrolled at a registered school at the time of competition.
How many events do teams compete in?
+
FFC features three main events: Business Debate, Case Study Analysis, and the Innovators' Challenge. These are spread across two competition days. Teams can participate in all three events.
Is AI allowed during the competition?
+
AI rules differ by event. In the Business Debate, ChatGPT is permitted only during the initial 15-minute research phase — final speeches must be original. In the Case Study Analysis, AI tools are strictly prohibited. In the Innovators' Challenge, AI may be used for research and calculations only — direct copying of AI-generated content is not permitted.
What awards are available?
+
FFC offers event-specific awards (Market Persuaders Award, Analytical Excellence Award, Future Founder Trophy), a Startup Award (Honourable Mention), and the Titan of Industry Award for the overall best team across all events.
Can certificates be used for university applications?
+
Yes. FFC provides recognised awards and certified achievements that enhance university and scholarship applications. Certificates are signed by POWIIS school leadership. Participation demonstrates leadership, innovation, and entrepreneurship to admissions officers.
How do I register?
+
Registration details will be announced closer to the competition date. Please check back on this website or contact the FFC team at our general enquiry email for updates.
